Build a premium, visually diverse, modern webpage for “Designforge Apprenticeship”.

Brand context:
Designforge is a mentorship-led design learning ecosystem for aspirants, students, and early professionals. This page is for the “Apprenticeship” initiative — focused on industry-ready programs, workshops, internship exposure, mentorship, and community-led design awareness.

Core intent of the page:
Show that Designforge Apprenticeship helps learners move beyond theory through practical design exposure, guided learning, industry training collaborations, and social impact.

Tone:
- warm
- credible
- mentorship-led
- future-ready
- socially conscious
- not like a coaching institute
- minimal and premium
- easy to scan

Very important UI direction:
Do NOT make every section a card grid.
Use a diverse mix of UI patterns so the page feels light, modern, and premium.

Use these UI patterns across the page:
- split hero layout
- statement strip
- editorial 2-column section
- horizontal topic chips / visual list
- 4-step timeline
- logo carousel
- image + text social impact section
- featured session + workshop list
- audience list / inline bands
- FAQ accordion
- centered CTA banner

Primary CTA:
Apply for Apprenticeship

Secondary CTA:
Join Upcoming Workshops

Tertiary CTA:
Partner With Us

GENERAL DESIGN NOTES
--------------------------------------------------
- avoid repeating card grids across the page

- premium typography and spacing
- modern, warm, and credible aesthetic
- highly readable with short content blocks
- responsive on desktop/tablet/mobile
- subtle animations only
- use placeholders for:
  - workshop visuals
  - mentors
  - collaboration logos
  - outreach/community images
- page should feel contemporary, human, and trust-worthy
- do not make it look like a generic course site or coaching page
